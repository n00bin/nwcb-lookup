Tell us about your toon

Paragon, race, item level. The three knobs that decide what your stats actually mean.

Ability scores

Your class sets the starting numbers. At levels 9 and 16 every ability gets a free +1. At the other six leveling-up points you pick two abilities and add +1 to each. Anything from gear or boons piles on top automatically. See Mekaniks → Abilities for what each ability score actually does in combat.

Equip your gear

Click any slot. The pickers know which class wears what. Empty slots are just wasted Item Level.

Enchantments

Eleven slots in total: 4 Offense, 4 Defense, 1 Utility, 1 Combat, and 1 Bonus. Universal enchants change what stat they give based on which slot you put them in.

Overloads

Two slots, each adds a flat percent bonus. You can't equip the same overload in both slots — once you pick it, it disappears from the second slot's picker.

Artifacts

The Primary artifact is the one with the active power you can press in a fight. The other three secondary artifacts don't have an active power — you equip them for their stat bonuses.

Bolster

Bolster is what your top 10 companions and top 10 mounts by quality contribute, even when they're not summoned. Type how many of each rarity you own and the engine computes your bolster automatically. See Mekaniks → Bolster for the per-tier table.

Mount stable

Five mounts in your stable. Each gets a collar, a mount, and a fistful of insignias. Pick which Insignia Bonus you want per mount — slot the matching insignia categories and the row turns green. Running the same bonus on multiple mounts stacks at 100% / 50% / 25% (see Mekaniks → Stacking).

Boons

Spend your campaign points. Use the +/− buttons on each row to add or remove points. Master tier boons cost more per rank but each rank gives you all three of its effects.

Guild boons

One pick per category — whatever your guild's stronghold has unlocked. Offense, Defense, Utility.

Optional buffs

Stuff you eat, drink, or smash before a fight. One per category. Always-on freebies sit at the bottom — uncheck the ones you don't actually have.

Run the math

Type your power's magnitude from the in-game tooltip. We'll run the math against a typical PvE enemy and show what one hit would actually do. See Mekaniks → Damage for the full formula.

💡 Chance = how likely each outcome is on a single hit. All the cards below add up to 100% — every hit has to land in exactly one.
