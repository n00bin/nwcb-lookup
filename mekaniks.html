Core Mechanics

Rating to Percentage
Each stat has a rating value (large numbers in thousands) that converts to a percentage. The conversion depends on your Total Item Level (TIL). The rating contribution alone is capped — you reach the total cap by combining rating with non-rating sources (companions, boons, percent items).
Rating contribution = 50 + (Rating - TIL) / 1000
Stat % total = Rating contribution + Percent Bonuses (up to total cap)
At rating = TIL50% contribution from rating
Every 1,000 rating above/below TIL±1% to rating contribution
Rating cap (90% total stats)50% — reached when rating = TIL
Rating cap (120% total stats)60% — reached when rating = TIL + 10,000
To reach total capAdd percent bonuses (companions, boons, items) — these are not rating-capped
Rule of thumb1 IL on gear ≈ 15 stat rating points
Example120k IL with 100k Deflect rating: 50 + (100k - 120k)/1k = 30% from rating
Combined Rating
Combined Rating is a single number that adds equally to ALL your core stats at once. It comes from companions, mounts, and certain gear.
Combined Rating adds its value to every core stat (Power, Defense, Crit Strike, etc.)
SourcesCompanions, mounts, insignias, companion gear
Example+2,700 CR = +2,700 to Power, Defense, Crit Strike, etc.
Why it mattersHigher CR items raise all stats, not just one

Combat Formulas

Base Damage & Base HP by Role
Base Damage and Base HP are derived from your Total Item Level (TIL) plus a role multiplier. Damage roles favor offense; HP favors defense. The multipliers are inverted between the two.
Base Damage
Base Damage = (TIL / 10) × role multiplier
DPS× 1.20
Healer× 1.10
Tank× 1.00
Example (44,632 IL DPS)4,463.2 × 1.2 = 5,355.84 base damage
Base HP
Base HP = (TIL × 10) × role multiplier
Tank× 1.20
Healer× 1.10
DPS× 1.00
Example (44,632 IL DPS)446,320 base HP (before CON, gear, buffs)
Magnitude
Every power has a magnitude number that determines how much damage or healing it does relative to your base damage. Higher magnitude = more damage.
Actual Damage = (Magnitude / 100) x Base Damage x Multipliers
Base DamageTIL / 10 (+ role bonus)
DPS Bonus+20% base damage
Healer Bonus+10% base damage
Example1,000 mag = 10x your base damage before multipliers
Damage Pipeline
Damage goes through multiple multipliers in order. Each stat multiplies the result independently.
Final Damage = Base x Power x CA x Crit x Buffs / Enemy Defense
Order of Operations
1. Base Damage(Magnitude / 100) x (TIL / 10)
2. Power Multiplierx (1 + Power %)
3. Combat Advantagex (1 + CA % - enemy Awareness %)
4. Critical StrikeIf crit: x (1 + Crit Sev % - enemy Crit Avoid %)
5. Buff/DebuffAll damage buffs/debuffs applied multiplicatively
6. Enemy Defense/ (1 + enemy Defense %)
7. Deflect CheckIf deflected: / (1 + Deflect Sev % - your Accuracy %)
Full Expected-Value Formula ▶
For theorycraft and DPS calculators. Most players don't need this — see the simplified Pipeline above. This is the canonical expected-value formula: average damage over many hits, accounting for crit chance × severity, CA uptime, deflect probability, and all the multiplicative damage-boost categories.
E[damage] = (IL / 10) × Role Bonus
          × (Magnitude / 100)
          × (1 + PowerTotal / 100)
          × (1 + (CritChanceTotal / 100) × ((CritSeverityTotal − TargetCritAvoidanceTotal) / 100))
          × (1 + (CombatAdvantageUpTime% / 100) × ((CombatAdvantageTotal − TargetAwareness) / 100))
          × (1 + TargetDeflectChance × (1 / (TargetDeflectSev − AccuracyTotal + 100) − 1 / 100))
          × (100 / (100 + TargetDefenseTotal − ΣDefenseDebuffs))
          × (1 + (ΣDamageBonuses% + OutgoingDamage%) / 100)
          × (1 + BaseDamageBoost / 100)
          × (1 + (MagicalDamageBoost or PhysicalDamageBoost) / 100)
          × (1 + (AtWill / Encounter / DailyDamageBoost) / 100)
          × Π (1 / TargetMitigation)
          × (1 / SpecialDebuffsOnPlayer)
          × (1 + ΣTargetDebuffsIn% / 100)
Variable Glossary
IL / 10 × Role BonusBase damage. Role multiplier: DPS ×1.2, Healer ×1.1, Tank ×1.0
MagnitudePower's listed magnitude (e.g. an at-will hitting for 200 magnitude)
PowerTotalYour final Power %, after rating + percent bonuses
CritChanceTotalFinal Critical Strike % (the chance to crit)
CritSeverityTotal − TargetCritAvoidanceEffective crit severity after target's mitigation
CombatAdvantageUpTime%How often you actually have CA (positioning matters — 100% if always flanking)
CombatAdvantageTotal − TargetAwarenessEffective CA bonus after target's mitigation
TargetDeflectChanceTarget's chance to deflect your hits (PvE enemies typically 50%)
TargetDeflectSev − AccuracyTotalReduction applied on deflected hits (your Accuracy lowers it)
TargetDefenseTotal − ΣDefenseDebuffsTarget's defense after all debuffs you've stacked on it
ΣDamageBonuses% + OutgoingDamage%Sum of generic damage % bonuses (companions, boons, gear) + role-scaled outgoing
BaseDamageBoostBonus to base damage from companion bonus powers and similar sources
Magical / Physical DamageBoostDamage-type-specific bonus (one or the other, depending on power)
AtWill / Encounter / Daily BoostPower-slot-specific bonus (separate multiplier per slot type)
Π (1 / TargetMitigation)Product over multiple target mitigation effects (e.g. damage resistance auras)
SpecialDebuffsOnPlayerDebuffs that scale your outgoing damage down (dazes, etc.)
ΣTargetDebuffsIn%Sum of "incoming damage to target" debuffs you've applied
Key insights from the formula
CA Uptime mattersCombat Advantage scales by your uptime — 50% uptime halves its effective value
Categories stack multiplicativelyDamageBonuses, BaseDamage, Magical/Physical, Slot-type, Target debuffs are all separate multipliers
Within a category: additiveTwo +10% damage bonuses in the same bucket = +20%, not ×1.21
Full Expected-Value Healing Formula ▶
For theorycraft and healing calculators. Average HP restored over many casts. Simpler than the damage formula — no defense, deflect, or target mitigation, since healing isn't reduced by defensive stats. Note that healing crits are half-effective: Critical Severity is divided by 2.
E[healing] = (IL / 10) × (Magnitude / 100)
           × (1 + PowerTotal / 100)
           × (1 + (CritChanceTotal / 100) × (CritSeverityTotal / (100 × 2)))
           × (1 + OutgoingHealing% / 100)
           × (1 + TargetIncomingHealing% / 100)
           × (1 + MultiplicativeExceptions / 100)
Variable Glossary
IL / 10Base healing. Healer role bonus (×1.1) applies analogously to damage's role bonus
MagnitudeHealing power's listed magnitude (same scale as damage powers)
PowerTotalYour final Power %, after rating + percent bonuses
CritChanceTotal × (CritSev / 200)Heal crit chance × half-effective crit severity (the /2 is the healer crit penalty)
OutgoingHealing%Your outgoing healing % (caps at 120%)
TargetIncomingHealing%Receiver's incoming healing % — stacks on top of your output
MultiplicativeExceptionsCatch-all for special healing buffs (power-specific multipliers)
Key insights from the formula
Crit severity is halvedHealers gain only 50% of their Crit Severity stat — Power and Outgoing Healing scale better point-for-point
Stack Incoming Healing on tanksIt multiplies on top of your output independently of your stats
No defensive interactionHeals aren't reduced by defense/deflect/mitigation — every cast lands at full math
Power and OH are equal-priorityBoth are flat multipliers in the formula — balance them rather than stacking one
Full Expected-Value EHP Formula ▶
Effective HP — how much damage can be applied to you before you die. Structurally the mirror of the damage formula from the defender's perspective: every per-hit damage multiplier becomes a divisor, and your defensive stats (Defense, Deflect, Crit Avoidance, Awareness) shrink those divisors so EHP grows. Useful for comparing survivability across builds.
Three EHP Cases
Minimum EHPWorst case — attacker crits, has CA, you don't deflect
Average EHPExpected value over many hits (this is the formula below)
Maximum EHPBest case — every defensive proc lands, no enemy crit/CA
E[EHP] = IL × 10 × Role Bonus
       ÷ (1 + (EnemyCritChance / 100) × ((EnemyCritSev − CritAvoidance) / 100))
       ÷ (1 + (EnemyCAUpTime% / 100) × ((EnemyCATotal − Awareness) / 100))
       ÷ (1 + DeflectChance × (1 / (DeflectSev − EnemyAccuracy + 100) − 1 / 100))
       ÷ (100 / (100 + DefenseTotal − ΣDefenseDebuffsOnPlayer))
       ÷ Π (1 / Mitigation)
       ÷ (1 / SpecialDebuffsOnPlayer)
Variable Glossary
IL × 10 × Role BonusBase HP. Role multiplier: Tank ×1.2, Healer ×1.1, DPS ×1.0
EnemyCritChance / EnemyCritSevAttacker's crit chance and crit severity (your Critical Avoidance subtracts from severity)
EnemyCAUpTime%How often the enemy has CA on you (positioning by your tank/group affects this)
EnemyCATotal − AwarenessEffective enemy CA after your Awareness mitigation
DeflectChanceYour deflect % (chance to deflect a hit)
DeflectSev − EnemyAccuracyReduction applied on deflected hits (enemy's Accuracy lowers your deflect mitigation)
DefenseTotal − ΣDefenseDebuffsOnPlayerYour defense after enemy debuffs reduce it
Π (1 / Mitigation)Product over multiple personal mitigation effects (damage resistance auras, shields, etc.)
1 / SpecialDebuffsOnPlayerSpecial debuffs that scale incoming damage on you up (vulnerability marks, etc.)
Key insights from the formula
Defensive stats compoundEach defensive stat is its own divisor — Defense, Awareness, Crit Avoid, Deflect all multiply EHP independently
Awareness > Defense above 45%Bosses have ~90% CA. Each point of Awareness directly subtracts from that 90% — high impact when stacked
Crit Avoidance prevents one-shotsBoss crits scale by enemy Crit Severity (often 100%+). Crit Avoidance subtracts directly — critical for tanks
EHP isn't HPAdding flat HP raises EHP linearly; raising defensive stats raises EHP multiplicatively. Stat investment usually beats HP at scale
Diminishing Returns
Because defensive stats use division formulas, each additional point of a stat is worth slightly less than the previous one. The stat still helps, but the benefit per point decreases.
Defense Example
0% Defense0% damage reduction
50% Defense~33% damage reduction
90% Defense~47% damage reduction
120% Defense~55% damage reduction
Damage Reduction = 1 - (1 / (1 + Defense %)). Going from 0 to 50% saves more than going from 50% to 120%.

Scaling

How Scaling Works
When you enter scaled content (older dungeons, trials), your stats are adjusted down to match the content's level. Understanding scaling is key to not losing effectiveness.
Key Rules
TIL in scaled contentDoesn't matter — stats are rescaled
What mattersStat percentages, not raw ratings
Percent bonusesMore valuable (boons, companion powers, etc.)
Utility statsAP Gain and Recharge Speed bypass scaling
The Scaling Trap
Items that give high IL but low stats (like collars or combat enchantments) can actually hurt you in scaled content. They raise your TIL without adding enough rating to match, which lowers your stat percentages after scaling.
In scaled content: prioritize stat efficiency over raw item level.

Stacking Rules

Multiplicative Stacking
Most damage buffs and debuffs in Neverwinter stack multiplicatively, not additively. This means each buff multiplies the result of the previous one.
+10% and +15% = 1.10 x 1.15 = 26.5% total (not 25%)
Damage buffsMultiplicative
Damage debuffs on enemiesMultiplicative
Defense statsMultiplicative (division formula)
Same-Source Stacking
When multiple party members use the same buff, the second instance usually provides reduced benefit.
Aura equip powers (e.g. Runic Aura)Second stack = 50% effectiveness
Spined Devil + SuccubusDo NOT stack (same debuff type)
Different buff sourcesStack fully
Consumable Stacking
One buff per category, but different categories stack with each other.
Elixir1 at a time (replaces previous)
Potion / Flask1 at a time (replaces previous)
Event Food1 at a time (replaces previous)
Stronghold Food1 at a time (replaces previous)
Invocation Blessing1 at a time (replaces previous)
Overload Enchantments1 per overload slot on gear
You can run an Elixir + Potion + Event Food + SH Food + Blessing + Overloads all at once.
